1. Who We Are

referme.help is operated by Robworks Software ("we," "us," "our"). We provide a HIPAA-compliant referral management platform that connects patients, referring providers, and specialist practices to streamline the medical referral process.

Depending on context, we operate as either a Covered Entity or a Business Associate under HIPAA:

  • Business Associate — when a healthcare provider (a Covered Entity) uses referme.help to manage referrals on behalf of their patients. In this context, we process Protected Health Information (PHI) under a Business Associate Agreement (BAA).
  • Third-Party Application — when patients directly authorize referme.help to access their health data via SMART on FHIR (e.g., connecting a MyChart account). In this context, the patient's explicit authorization governs the disclosure per 45 CFR §164.502(a)(1)(iv) and ONC's Information Blocking regulations (21st Century Cures Act).

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

Name, email address, password (bcrypt-hashed — never stored in plaintext), professional role, and organization affiliation. For GitHub OAuth users: GitHub username and publicly-scoped email.

2.2 Patient Information (PHI)

When you create or manage referrals, we collect and store:

  • Patient name, date of birth, contact information, and Medical Record Number (MRN)
  • Diagnoses (ICD-10 codes and descriptions)
  • Current medications and dosages
  • Allergies and adverse reactions
  • Insurance plan and payer information
  • Clinical notes, referral reason, and clinical questions
  • Authorization numbers and prior authorization status
  • Referral documents and file attachments

All of the above constitutes Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA and is handled accordingly.

2.3 EHR-Imported Information

When you authorize referme.help to connect to your Electronic Health Record via SMART on FHIR, we may import: patient demographics, problem list / active diagnoses, medication requests, and allergy records. All imported data is subject to the same PHI protections as manually-entered data and is used exclusively to pre-populate your referral record.

2.4 Usage & Technical Information

IP address, browser user-agent (truncated at 500 characters), session identifiers, and referral workflow events collected by our platform. We use Google Analytics 4 (property ID G-7LGDP4Z19X, IP anonymization enabled) for aggregate, anonymized usage analytics on unauthenticated pages only (home, search, documentation, and legal pages). GA4 is not loaded on authenticated pages where Protected Health Information may be present. GA4 data is not linked to individual patient records.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use information collected for the following purposes:

  • Providing, operating, and improving the referme.help platform
  • Processing and tracking medical referrals on your behalf
  • Sending referral packets via fax or secure email to specialist practices
  • Running real-time insurance eligibility verification (via Availity)
  • Generating and delivering referral documents (PDF, FHIR)
  • Maintaining audit logs for compliance and accountability
  • Communicating with you about your account and referrals
  • Detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, and security incidents
  • Complying with applicable law, including HIPAA

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or PHI to any third party for marketing purposes. We do not use PHI for advertising.

4. HIPAA — Protected Health Information

4.1 Minimum Necessary Standard

We apply the HIPAA Minimum Necessary standard (45 CFR §164.502(b)) to all PHI access, use, and disclosure. Workforce members and automated systems access only the PHI necessary for their specific role or function.

4.2 Permitted Uses and Disclosures of PHI

We may use or disclose PHI without patient authorization only as permitted by HIPAA:

  • Treatment: transmitting referral packets to receiving specialists
  • Operations: internal quality improvement, audit, and staff training
  • Payment: eligibility verification and authorization coordination
  • As required by law: court orders, subpoenas, public health reporting
  • Business Associates: sub-processors under BAA (see Section 11)

4.3 Uses Requiring Authorization

We will not use or disclose PHI for marketing, sale of PHI, or any purpose not described above without your explicit written authorization. You may revoke any authorization at any time by contacting privacy@referme.help.

4.4 Business Associate Agreements

Healthcare providers and organizations using referme.help to manage patient referrals must execute a BAA with Robworks Software before processing PHI. To request a BAA, contact privacy@referme.help. We maintain executed BAAs with all sub-processors who handle PHI (listed in Section 11).

4.5 HIPAA Privacy Officer

Our designated HIPAA Privacy Officer can be reached at privacy@referme.help.

5. EHR & SMART on FHIR Integration

Feature status: EHR / SMART on FHIR integration is currently in development (Phase 12). The practices described in this section will apply once the feature is generally available. If you have questions, contact privacy@referme.help.

5.1 Patient-Authorized Data Access

When the EHR integration feature is available, referme.help will support connecting to your Electronic Health Record (e.g., Epic MyChart) via the SMART App Launch Framework (v2.0). When you initiate this connection:

  • You will be redirected to your EHR provider's login and consent screen
  • You explicitly authorize which data referme.help may access
  • We will receive only the FHIR resources you approved (Patient, Condition, MedicationRequest, AllergyIntolerance)
  • This authorization constitutes a patient-directed disclosure under 45 CFR §164.502(a)(1)(iv)

5.2 Token Storage

OAuth 2.0 access tokens received from your EHR will be encrypted at rest before storage in our database. Tokens will be invalidated when you request disconnection (by contacting privacy@referme.help) or upon account deletion. You may also revoke access directly in your EHR's patient portal at any time.

5.3 PKCE Security

All EHR OAuth flows will use Proof Key for Code Exchange (PKCE, RFC 7636). No client secret is transmitted or stored. This prevents authorization code interception attacks.

5.4 Data Use After Import

Health data imported from your EHR will be used solely to pre-populate referral forms on your behalf. It will not be shared with third parties beyond what is necessary to deliver your referral (e.g., the receiving specialist practice). You may delete imported data at any time by contacting privacy@referme.help.

5.5 Information Blocking

In accordance with the 21st Century Cures Act and ONC Information Blocking regulations (45 CFR Part 171), referme.help does not engage in information blocking. We support patients' right to access their own health information through standardized APIs.

6. How We Share Information

We share information only in the following circumstances:

6.1 Referral Delivery

Referral packets (containing PHI) are transmitted to the receiving specialist practice via fax (Documo), secure email (Resend), or direct messaging as directed by you. Each transmission is logged in the audit trail.

6.2 Sub-Processors (Business Associates)

See Section 11 for the full list of sub-processors and their functions.

6.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of referme.help, our users, or the public.

6.4 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, PHI will be transferred only subject to equivalent privacy protections and applicable HIPAA requirements. We will notify affected users and update this policy.

6.5 No Sale of PHI

We never sell, rent, or trade Protected Health Information. This prohibition is absolute.

7. Your Rights

7.1 HIPAA Rights (for patients whose PHI we hold)

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your PHI we hold (45 CFR §164.524). We will respond within 30 days.
  • Right to Amend: Request correction of inaccurate PHI (45 CFR §164.526). We will respond within 60 days.
  • Right to Accounting of Disclosures: Request a list of disclosures of your PHI made outside of treatment, payment, and operations (45 CFR §164.528), covering up to 6 years.
  • Right to Restrict: Request restrictions on certain uses or disclosures (45 CFR §164.522). We will consider all requests in good faith.
  • Right to Confidential Communications: Request PHI be communicated via alternative means or locations.
  • Right to Complain: File a complaint with us or with the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services Office for Civil Rights (OCR) at hhs.gov/hipaa/filing-a-complaint. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

7.2 Account Rights (all users)

  • Access & Correction: View and update your account information at any time from your profile page.
  • Data Export: Request a machine-readable export of your account data by contacting privacy@referme.help.
  • Account Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated data. PHI required to be retained for legal or regulatory purposes will be retained only for the minimum required period.
  • EHR Disconnect: When the EHR integration feature is active, revoke referme.help's access by contacting privacy@referme.help or by revoking access directly in your EHR's patient portal (e.g., Epic MyChart → Apps).

To exercise any of these rights, contact privacy@referme.help. We will verify your identity before processing requests involving PHI.

8. Security Measures

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards consistent with HIPAA Security Rule requirements (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C):

8.1 Technical Safeguards

  • All data transmitted over TLS 1.2 or higher (TLS 1.3 preferred)
  • Passwords hashed with bcrypt (cost factor ≥12)
  • EHR OAuth tokens will be encrypted at rest when the EHR integration feature ships
  • Server-side session management with cryptographically random session tokens
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with a six-level permission ladder
  • Append-only audit log for all PHI access and modification events
  • File attachments virus-scanned before storage (Cloudmersive)
  • Private object storage with signed URLs (15-minute expiry) for attachment access
  • Rate limiting on authentication and eligibility endpoints
  • Webhook signatures verified via HMAC-SHA256 before processing

8.2 Administrative Safeguards

  • Designated HIPAA Privacy Officer and Security Officer
  • Workforce access limited to minimum necessary for job function
  • Business Associate Agreements with all sub-processors handling PHI
  • Regular security reviews and vulnerability assessments

8.3 Physical Safeguards

referme.help is hosted on cloud infrastructure (Railway / Supabase) with SOC 2 Type II certification. We do not operate our own physical data centers. Physical access controls are maintained by our infrastructure providers.

8.4 Reporting Security Issues

To report a security vulnerability, contact security@referme.help. We will acknowledge receipt within 24 hours and provide updates on remediation.

9. Data Retention

We retain PHI for a minimum of 7 years from the date of creation (the default for medical records under most state laws and CMS Conditions of Participation), unless a longer retention period is required by applicable law or your organization's policy. Organizations may configure retention up to 30 years for pediatric or other specialized records.

Non-PHI account data (name, email, login history) is retained for the duration of your account and for 90 days after deletion to allow recovery.

Audit log entries are retained for a minimum of 6 years per HIPAA requirements (45 CFR §164.530(j)).

After expiry, data is soft-deleted (marked deleted in the database) and then permanently purged in a nightly automated sweep.

12. Children's Privacy

referme.help accounts may only be created by individuals 18 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect account information from minors.

Referrals involving pediatric patients (under 18) are treated as PHI and handled under standard HIPAA protections. Where applicable, special protections for minor patient records under state law are honored. Some states grant minors independent privacy rights for certain categories of care (e.g., mental health, reproductive health) — providers are responsible for complying with applicable state minor consent laws when submitting referrals.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected account information from a minor, contact privacy@referme.help immediately.

13. State-Specific Rights

13.1 California (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect about you.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., legal compliance, HIPAA retention requirements).
  • Right to Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

Note: PHI subject to HIPAA is exempt from CCPA per Civil Code §1798.145(c)(1)(A). The rights above apply to non-PHI personal information.

To submit a CCPA request, contact privacy@referme.help with subject line "CCPA Request." We will respond within 45 days.

13.2 Other States

Various other states have enacted or are enacting comprehensive privacy laws (Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, Texas TDPSA, and others). Where applicable, residents of those states may exercise similar rights by contacting privacy@referme.help. We will honor applicable state law rights to the extent they apply to non-PHI personal information.

14. Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will comply with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D):

  • Notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay and within 60 days of discovery
  • Notify the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS) within 60 days for breaches affecting 500 or more individuals, or annually for smaller breaches
  • Notify prominent media outlets in affected states for breaches of 500 or more residents of that state
  • Notify our Business Associate counterparts (Covered Entities) within 30 days of discovery

To report a suspected breach or security incident, contact security@referme.help immediately.

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy and HIPAA Notice from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via:

  • Email notification to account holders
  • A prominent notice on this page for at least 30 days
  • Updated "Last updated" date at the top of this document

Continued use of referme.help after the effective date of changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not agree, you must discontinue use and may request deletion of your data.
